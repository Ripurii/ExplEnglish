intro: "In this lesson, students familiarize themselves with the grammar subject 'relative pronouns' while simultaneously identifying cultural aspects of British culture and art in a playful manner and familiarizing themselves with stereotypes. This cross-curricular lesson plan entails art and citizenship lesson goals as well."
learning_goals:
  - Students can identify the main idea and key details in an informational text about Comfort Blanket.
  - Students can identify and explain at least five British cultural symbols represented in Comfort Blanket.
  - Students can recognise and use the relative pronouns who, which and that.
  - Students can write a museum label explaining how a collage represents a country's identity.
  - Students can express their choices about their work in English.
  - Students can ask and answer questions about a classmate's artwork during the gallery walk.
  - Students can use appropriate interaction strategies, such as asking for clarification and responding to follow-up questions.
cross_curricular:
  - subject: "Art"
    goals:
      - Students can analyse how visual symbols communicate meaning.
      - Students can create a collage that communicates an idea about national identity by intentionally using colours, shapes, composition and visual symbols.
      - Students can present and reflect on their own artwork and the artwork of others.
  - subject: "Citizenship"
    goals:
      - Students can reflect on how artworks can represent identity, diversity and stereotypes.
      - Students can discuss different perspectives on national identity respectfully and with evidence.
lesson_phases:
  phases:
    - label: "Warm-up"
      goal: "Activate prior knowledge and introduce the topic."
      items:
        - "Give each student a Post-it note."
        - "Without showing Comfort Blanket, ask students to draw as many things as they associate with Britain as possible."
        - "Compare drawings in pairs and briefly discuss as a class."
        - "Reveal Grayson Perry's Comfort Blanket."
        - "Students identify and count recognisable British symbols they can find in the artwork - use 'What do you recognize?'"
    - label: "Orientation"
      goal: "Introduce the lesson focus."
      items:
        - "Introduce the learning goals: analyse an artwork about British identity; practise who, which and that; create a collage and museum label."
    - label: "Pre-reading"
      goal: "Prepare students for reading."
      items:
        - "Predict what the article will discuss based on the artwork."
        - "Introduce or revise key vocabulary."
    - label: "While Reading"
      goal: "Read for understanding and notice language."
      items:
        - "Students read the article individually."
        - "Complete reading comprehension questions."
        - "Read the text a second time and underline examples of who, which and that."
        - "In pairs, determine what each pronoun refers to (person or thing) - use two different colours."
    - label: "Post-reading"
      goal: "Consolidate understanding and practise grammar."
      items:
        - "Discuss the reading answers as a class."
        - "Elicit the rule for relative pronouns."
        - "Complete a short exercise combining sentences using who, which and that."
    - label: "Guided Practice"
      goal: "Consolidate students' understanding of relative pronouns through controlled grammar practice."
      items:
        - "Complete a multiple-choice exercise by choosing the correct relative pronoun (who, which or that) for each sentence. Justify each answer by briefly explaining why the chosen pronoun is correct."
        - "Complete a sentence-combining exercise, joining two simple sentences into one using a relative pronoun."
        - "Complete a sentence completion exercise by adding a relative clause about a cultural symbol, person or place."
    - label: "Application (Homework: Collage)"
      goal: "Apply knowledge and skills in a meaningful context."
      items:
        - "Create a collage representing national identity using colours, shapes, composition and visual symbols - for a country of their choosing."
        - "Write a museum label using target vocabulary and relative pronouns."
    - label: "Presentation"
      goal: "Present and justify artistic choices while evaluating different representations of national identity."
      items:
        - "Divide the class into two groups. One group stands next to their collage and museum label as museum guides, while the other group acts as museum visitors."
        - "Visitors walk around the exhibition in small groups. Each student presents their artwork several times (about 2 minutes per visitor group) and answers questions about their artistic choices."
        - "Visitors ask at least one question and provide brief verbal feedback before moving to the next artwork."
        - "After 15-20 minutes, the groups switch roles so that every student has presented their work and visited the exhibition."
        - "At the end of the gallery walk, each student individually selects four collages they believe should be included in the museum's exhibition 'National Identity.' In small groups, students compare their choices and agree on a shared shortlist of four artworks."
        - "Each group briefly explains why they selected these four collages, justifying their decisions with evidence from the presentations, visual design, and museum labels."
        - "Conclude with a whole-class reflection on why different groups made different selections and how national identity can be represented in multiple ways."
    - label: "Reflection (Exit Ticket)"
      goal: "Reflect on different representations of national identity and justify personal choices using relative pronouns."
      items:
        - "Students complete an individual Museum Curator Exit Ticket before leaving the classroom."
